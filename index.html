Bergen Bryggen

Bergen is one of the biggest citiest in Norway. The city was founded around 1070. In the picture you can see Bergen Bryggen. Bryggen meaning 'the dock' in Norwegian is a series of picturesque Hanseatic buildings lining the harbour. It is part of UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1979. In Bryggen you can visit historic dock houses museums, shops, restaurants and pubs.

Oslo

Oslo is the capital and largest city of Norway. This city combines city life with nature. Today is Oslo famous for modern design and architecture. Oslo Opera House is one of building standing out from the crowd. You can walk on its rooftop and see surounding nature and buildings. It is also great place to explore food, stunning scenety, healthy living and many oportunities for sport.

Solastranda

Solastranda (Sola Beach) is more than two kilometers long beach full of sand. It is located right next to Stavanger Airport. The beach is a popular place for kiting and surfing on windy days all-year-around and for swimming during summer. On windy and cloudy days it is very cold place.

Preikestolen

Preikestolen is one of the most visited tourist attraction in Norway. It is steep cliff which rises about 600 meters above Lysefjorden. It has almost flat top of 25 by 25 meters. During summer is can be overcrowded.

Trolltunga

Trolltunga means Troll tongue. This cliff is about 700 meters above lake of Ringedalsvatenet. This cliff juts horizontally out of mountain resembles a protruding tongue. The hike to the tongue is about 27 km long on rough terrain. It was long but not as terrible as it is presented.
